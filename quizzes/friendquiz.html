Thinking of asking a friend for advice? Think again!

Take this awesome quiz to learn who can help you reach your goals!

Posted on April 1, 2018 by Elena Agapie

Whom should you ask for help to get you started with exercise?

https://flic.kr/p/rqnjT

Correct!

Wrong!

Ask a friend!

We have found that friends tend to make recommendations that are close to what you are already doing (Agapie 2016). This can give you a good start to slowly ease into exercising. If you ask someone who doesn’t know you well they might push you to do more than you are ready for.

Whom should you ask for fashion advice?

https://flic.kr/p/bsJ5wa

Correct!

Wrong!

Ask a stranger!

Someone who doesn’t know you is more likely to give you honest advice about things they don’t like, e.g.,when you are making a fashion mistake. Studies show that when people ask for feedback on fashion choices online, strangers are more honest about what clothing they do not recommend for the person (Morris 2014).

Whom should you ask to help you reach your goals, if you worry about being judged?

https://flic.kr/p/dQG4qM

Correct!

Wrong!

Ask a stranger!

We found that people are concerned about being judged by their friends if they have to tell friends about their habits when trying to accomplish a personal goal like eating healthier, exercising, or saving money (Agapie 2016).

Whom should you ask for new ideas to help you reach your goals?

https://flic.kr/p/aBZZuB

Correct!

Wrong!

Ask a stranger!

Research shows that people who are in the same social network have more similar ideas than those outside of their network (Miller 2001). When asking for advice on exercising, eating healthier, or saving money, strangers provide more novel ideas (Agapie 2016).

Whom should you ask for help if you want them to check up on you?

https://flic.kr/p/22PuVY5

Correct!

Wrong!

Ask a friend!

We found that friends are interested in providing people with support in helping them reach their goals. They look forward to cooking meals together or exercising together in the future (Agapie 2016).
